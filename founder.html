Our Founder

The vision behind NexaTech

Founder Photo

Sarah Chen

Founder & CEO

With over 12 years of experience in the digital industry, Sarah Chen founded NexaTech with a clear vision: to bridge the gap between cutting-edge technology and business growth. Her passion for creating digital solutions that make a real impact has been the driving force behind the company's success.

Sarah's journey began in web development, where she quickly recognized the transformative power of well-executed digital strategies. Her expertise spans across web development, digital marketing, and user experience design, giving her a unique holistic perspective on digital transformation.

Professional Background

  • Former Lead Developer at TechSolutions Inc. (5 years)
  • Digital Strategy Consultant for Fortune 500 companies
  • Speaker at international tech conferences including Web Summit and TechCrunch Disrupt
  • Mentor for aspiring digital professionals through various mentorship programs
  • Published author on digital transformation and user experience design

Education & Certifications

  • MSc in Computer Science from Stanford University
  • Google Certified Professional - Digital Marketing
  • AWS Certified Solutions Architect
  • Certified Scrum Master (CSM)

Philosophy & Approach

"Great digital solutions shouldn't be reserved for large corporations with massive budgets. Every business, regardless of size, deserves access to technology that can transform their operations and accelerate their growth. At NexaTech, we're committed to making exceptional digital solutions accessible and affordable."

Sarah believes in a client-first approach, where understanding the client's business objectives and challenges is paramount. This philosophy has shaped NexaTech's culture of deep collaboration and tailored solutions.

Awards & Recognition

  • Forbes 30 Under 30 - Technology (2022)
  • Women in Tech Leadership Award (2021)
  • Digital Innovation Award - Business Transformation (2020)
  • Top 100 Digital Influencers - TechCrunch (2019)
